The Social Pillar: Human Capital & Equity

The Cost in the Classroom

Lack of menstrual health support is a primary driver of school absenteeism, directly hindering educational attainment and future opportunities for girls.

The Productivity Drain

Poor menstrual health leads to significant economic losses through absenteeism and presenteeism. A Dutch study found an average loss of 8.9 productivity days per year per woman.

The Economic Burden of Neglect

Addressing women's health is not just a social good; it's a massive economic opportunity. The cost of inaction is measured in the tens of billions annually for individual nations.
